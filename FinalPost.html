Jacob Klein's Final Blog Post

12/14/2021

If I were trying to teach this course I would have more teaching through live demonstrations. Its no secret that most students don't do the readings and when they do, its not assured that they will be following along live either. Because of this, I would say having smaller projects that maybe don't even do anything but allow the student to create their own skeleton of a project would be beneficial. Then you could have the homework assignment to be to use the skeleton they created in class to make something personal and creative.

The things that helped me the most in the course is seeing a working version and being able to mess with it. Unfortunately, for a new javascript coder nothing seemed consistent or logical with previous languages. This made even the basics very confusing and I would often get hung up. Seeing someone else do it meant that I could get my code working and not get left behind from the rest of the class while also being able to go back later and change things and understand what does and doesn't work.
